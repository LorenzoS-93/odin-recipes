Spezzatino

The king of second courses

If in Italian cuisine it is easy to get lost in discussions about which is the main first course on Sunday, for the second courses there is only one answer: his majesty the stew. Its slow cooking and its few but essential aromas make it a recipe that is impossible not to love!

Ingredients

  • Beef (priest's hat) 1 kg
  • Beef broth approximately 2.5 l
  • Onion 1
  • Carrot 1
  • Leg of celery 1
  • Butter 30 g
  • 00 flour 30 g
  • 4 tablespoons EVO oil
  • Red wine 1 glass
  • Rormarino 1 sprig
  • Thyme 1 sprig
  • Sage 3 leaves
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

  1. First we prepare the meat broth and leave it warm
  2. Clean the celery, onion and carrot and chop them into cubes no more than 5 mm thick. We combine rosemary, thyme and sage. Finally, we cut the meat into cubes of approximately 5 cm. It is advisable to have this done by a competent butcher.
  3. In a tall pan, heat the butter with the oil, when the bottom is hot, add the chopped vegetables and leave season over a low heat for about 10 minutes, add a little hot broth as needed.
  4. When the vegetables are well thickened, add the meat, raise the heat and let the meat seal well for about 10 minutes. then season with salt and pepper.
  5. Sprinkle with flour and leave to toast for a few minutes, continuing to move the meat so that the flour does not burn. Then add the wine and wait for it to evaporate completely, then add the fragrant bunch.
  6. Then cover the meat with the broth and cover with the lid and leave to cook for about 2 hours over medium heat, taking care stir occasionally and add broth if necessary.
  7. Once cooking is finished, remove the fragrant bunch and add salt if necessary, your stew is ready!
